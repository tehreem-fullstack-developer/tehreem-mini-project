Fruits Festivals

Cherry Blossom Festival (Japan)


Late March to early April, depending on the region and bloom.


While not exclusively a fruit festival, it's a celebration of the cherry blossoms' bloom in spring, which coincides with cherry fruit season. It typically occurs from late March to early May.Celebrates the blooming of cherry blossoms, with festivals in Tokyo, Kyoto, and other regions. While not exclusively a fruit festival, the season aligns with cherry fruit harvesting. Events include picnics under blooming cherry trees, traditional performances, and light displays.


Dole Pineapple Festival - Hawaii, USA


Early July (dates can vary).


Held in Wahiawa, Oahu. The festival celebrates Hawaii’s pineapple industry with food and drink vendors, live entertainment, and pineapple-themed activities.


Pomegranate Festival - Iran


Usually in late October or November.


Celebrated in Kerman and other regions, the festival highlights the cultural and culinary importance of pomegranates, featuring local dishes, music, and traditional ceremonies.

Mango Festival - India:


Early September.


The Mango Festival in New Delhi is a vibrant celebration of the king of fruits, featuring a wide variety of mangoes, mango-themed dishes, and culinary competitions.


Fruit Harvest Festival - China:


Typically in June.


Various regions in China hold fruit harvest festivals, including the Yangmei Festival in Zhejiang Province, celebrating the harvest of the Yangmei fruit with local foods, games, and cultural performances.
